Understanding DMARC, SPF & DKIM

ðŸ›¡ï¸ What is DMARC?

DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting and Conformance) is an email security protocol that helps protect your domain from email spoofing and phishing attacks.

DMARC builds on two existing authentication methods (SPF and DKIM) and tells receiving mail servers what to do with emails that fail authentication checks.

  • Protect your brand: Prevent attackers from sending fake emails that appear to come from your domain
  • Improve deliverability: Legitimate emails are more likely to reach inboxes
  • Gain visibility: Receive reports showing who is sending email on behalf of your domain

ðŸ“§ What is SPF?

SPF (Sender Policy Framework) allows you to specify which mail servers are authorized to send email for your domain.

How it works: You publish an SPF record in DNS listing approved IP addresses. When an email arrives, the receiving server checks if it came from an approved IP.

Results you'll see:

  • PASS - Email came from an authorized server
  • FAIL - Email came from an unauthorized server (possible spoofing)
  • SOFTFAIL - Probably not authorized, but domain owner isn't certain
  • NONE - No SPF record found for the domain

ðŸ” What is DKIM?

DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail) adds a digital signature to your emails to verify they haven't been tampered with during transit.

How it works: Your mail server adds an encrypted signature to each email's headers. The receiving server uses your public key (published in DNS) to verify the signature.

Results you'll see:

  • PASS - Signature is valid, email hasn't been altered
  • FAIL - Signature is invalid or missing (possible tampering)
  • NONE - No DKIM signature was found

ðŸ“Š Reading Your Dashboard

Pass Rate: Percentage of emails passing BOTH SPF and DKIM. A high pass rate (>95%) indicates healthy email authentication.

Fail Rate: Emails failing BOTH checks. Investigate high fail rates - could indicate spoofing attempts or configuration issues.

Disposition: What the receiver did with the email:

  • None: Delivered normally (monitoring mode)
  • Quarantine: Sent to spam/junk folder
  • Reject: Blocked completely

  • Hover over badges to see detailed explanations of pass/fail reasons
  • Click "View" on any report to see detailed authentication records
  • Filter by source IP to investigate specific senders
  • Watch your pass rate: Sudden drops may indicate problems
  • Start with monitoring: Set DMARC policy to "none" initially to gather data without blocking legitimate email
